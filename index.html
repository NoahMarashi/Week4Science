Different types of Deserts

The first type of desert are Hot Deserts. Hot Deserts usually have high tempatures and low precipitation.
Hot deserts vary to be 20 to 25 degrees Celcius!

The Sahara desert is an example of a hot desert.

Another desert is the Semiarid deserts!
Semiarid deserts tempatures tend to be 10 to 38 degrees Celcius.
Semiarid deserts still have simalar conditions to hot deserts.


Coastal deserts are deserts with colder tempatures from 13 to 24 degrees Celcius.
Cold Deserts are deserts that are very simalar to Costal Deserts, but with more precipitation and snowfall rates.

The Desert I'm Doing today

The desert I'm doing today is the Kalahari Desert.

The Kalahari Desert is a desert in Africa in the countries Namibia, Botswana, and South Africa.
The average temperature for the Kalahari Desert (In the summer) is about 20 to 40 ℃.
The Desert’s average precipitation is about 5 to 10 inches every year.
It is the 4th largest desert in the world.

There are not that many things that are non-living in the desert
.The only few are rocks, sand, air, and water.
The producers in this desert are grasses, cacti, and small trees.
There are only a few Decomposers. Those consist of beetles, earthworms, and millipedes.

The sun helps plants feed themselves using photosynthesis.
Primary consumers eat those plants for energy.
Then, secondary consumers eat primary consumers.
When a plant/animal dies, a decomposer can recycle it to make it nutrients.
